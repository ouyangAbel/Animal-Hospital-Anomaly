Static Photo

How to detect and counter the Static Photo anomaly in Animal Hospital Anomaly. Photo Camera detection, patch 6/22 changes, and counter-strategy.

Photo
July 1, 2026

The Static Photo anomaly is one of the six Photo-Class anomalies in Animal Hospital Anomaly. It presents as a photo that comes back with TV-like static obscuring the image. This guide covers how to detect Static Photo, the important 6/22/26 patch change, and the best counter-strategy.

What Is the Static Photo Anomaly?

When you take a photo of the patient using the Photo Camera, the resulting image is obscured by TV-like static. The static covers the image, making it difficult or impossible to see the patient clearly. The static is the visual tell -- it indicates an anomaly.

Viewing the photo costs 10 sanity per view. The static itself is the anomaly indicator, so you don't need to see through it to know something is wrong.

Important: Patch 6/22/26 Change

As of the June 22, 2026 patch, static-only patients are now considered legitimate (not anomalies). This means that if the photo shows ONLY static (with no other anomaly tells), the patient is safe to admit. The static anomaly only applies when the static is accompanied by other suspicious signs.

How to Detect Static Photo

  • Snap a photo -- Take a photo of the patient at the admission window.
  • Look for static -- The photo should be obscured by TV-like static.
  • Check for other tells -- After the 6/22 patch, static alone isn't an anomaly. Look for additional signs.
  • Consider context -- If the patient looks suspicious at the window AND the photo is static, it's likely an anomaly.

What Happens If You Miss Static Photo?

If you admit a patient with the Static Photo anomaly (when it IS an anomaly), they will transform into a Skinwalker on the hospital floor.

Counter-Strategy

The best counter is context-aware photo detection. When the patient appears at the admission window:

  1. Complete the visual check first
  2. Snap a photo with the Photo Camera
  3. If the photo is static, check for other anomaly tells
  4. Consider the patient's overall appearance
  5. If suspicious, reject the patient to be safe

Shift Appearances

Static Photo can appear starting in Shift 2 and continues through higher shifts.
