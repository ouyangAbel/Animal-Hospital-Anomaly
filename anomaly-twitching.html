Twitching

How to detect and counter the Twitching anomaly in Animal Hospital Anomaly. Visual warning signs, CCTV variant, and rejection strategy.

Visual
July 1, 2026

The Twitching anomaly is one of the six Visual-Class anomalies in Animal Hospital Anomaly. It presents as a patient who jerks their arms, neck, or upper body at the admission window. This guide covers how to detect Twitching, the CCTV-only variant, and the best counter-strategy.

What Is the Twitching Anomaly?

The Twitching anomaly involves a patient who exhibits jerky, involuntary movements at the admission window. Watch the patient for 4-5 seconds -- if they jerk their arms, neck, or upper body, this is a Twitching anomaly. The movements are sudden and uncontrolled, like electric shocks running through the patient's body.

There is also a CCTV-only variant called Camera Twitching, where the patient appears normal at the window but twitches on the security camera feed. This variant requires checking the CCTV monitor to detect.

How to Detect Twitching (Visual)

  • Watch for 4-5 seconds -- Don't just glance at the patient. Spend a few seconds observing.
  • Look for jerking movements -- Arms, neck, or upper body should jerk suddenly and involuntarily.
  • Note the pattern -- Twitching is erratic and unpredictable, not rhythmic or controlled.
  • Compare to normal patients -- Normal patients stand relatively still.

How to Detect Camera Twitching (CCTV Variant)

  • Open the CCTV monitor -- This variant is only visible on the security camera feed.
  • Watch for jerking movements on the screen -- The patient will twitch on the monitor even though they look normal at the window.
  • Compare window and monitor views -- If the patient looks normal at the window but twitches on the monitor, it's Camera Twitching.

What Happens If You Miss Twitching?

If you admit a patient with the Twitching anomaly, they will transform into a Skinwalker on the hospital floor. The Skinwalker is the most dangerous enemy in the game -- it will attack you and other staff members, dealing bite damage and draining sanity.

Counter-Strategy

The best counter is prevention. When the patient appears at the admission window:

  1. Watch the patient for 4-5 seconds -- don't rush the check
  2. Look for jerking movements in the arms, neck, or upper body
  3. Open the CCTV monitor to check for Camera Twitching
  4. If you see twitching (visual or CCTV), reject the patient immediately
  5. The rejection is clean -- no sanity cost, no consequences

Why Twitching Is Easy to Miss

Twitching is easy to miss because it requires patience. Most players glance at the patient and move on, but Twitching only becomes visible if you watch for several seconds. The jerking movements are sudden and brief -- you might miss them if you look away at the wrong moment.

The CCTV variant is even trickier because you need to check the monitor in addition to the visual check. Make it a habit to always open the CCTV feed for every patient, even if the visual check looks clean.
