Animal Hospital Anomaly Best Class Guide: Complete Tier List & Progression (2026)

Every playable class ranked, priced, and explained — from free starters to Robux-exclusive endgame builds. Plus the optimal progression path for new players.

Class Guide
July 1, 2026 | 10 min read

Your class choice in Animal Hospital (Anomaly) defines everything: how you manage sanity, what tools you carry, how fast you move through the hospital, and how much punishment you can take before a shift collapses. Pick wrong and you're fighting the game with an unnecessary handicap. Pick right and mechanics that crushed you as an Intern become routine.

This Animal Hospital Anomaly class guide covers all 10 playable classes — free, coin-unlocked, and Robux-exclusive. We rank each class from S to D tier, break down costs and abilities, and map the optimal progression path from your first shift to endgame.

S-Tier Classes — The Best in the Game

Secret Agent (320 Robux) — The Combat God

Secret Agent starts every shift with a 20-use Gun. Twenty shots. That's enough to neutralize every Skinwalker, Hider, and hostile encounter in an entire shift without ever needing to find a Fire Extinguisher or Taser. The downside — friendly-fire risk is real — barely matters in solo play, and the damage output makes the game extremely easy even on Shift 5+. If you're willing to spend Robux and want to see all the content without friction, Secret Agent is the answer.

Best for: Solo players who want to cruise through all 5 shifts. Combat-heavy shift runs. Speedrunning.

Surgeon (2,500 Coins) — The Sustain Machine

Surgeon is widely considered the strongest free class in Animal Hospital Anomaly, and for good reason. Every successful cure grants both sanity restoration AND a temporary speed boost. This creates a self-compounding loop: the more patients you treat correctly, the saner and faster you get. A clean run with Surgeon becomes nearly untouchable by mid-shift. The 2,500 coin price tag means you'll need to grind, but it's worth every coin. Pair with the DNA Analysis shop upgrade to maximize patient identification speed.

Best for: All shift types. Players who've mastered the treatment workflow and can chain clean cures. Endgame push to Shift 5+.

A-Tier Classes — Strong Alternatives

Head Nurse (190 Robux) — The Premium Throughput Skip

Head Nurse starts with +3 inventory slots from Shift 1 — the same bonus you'd normally need to unlock the Extra Inventory Pouch shop upgrade. This is a massive quality-of-life boost. More slots mean you can carry Maple Syrup, Eye Drops, Fire Extinguisher, Coffee, AND treatment items simultaneously, eliminating most of the frantic inventory juggling that defines early shifts. At 190 Robux, it's the cheapest Robux option and arguably the best value for money.

Best for: New players who want a smoother learning curve. Players struggling with inventory management.

Doctor (Coins) — The Sanity Anchor

Doctor's passive restores 1 sanity per correctly treated patient, with bonus starting sanity at higher tiers. In a busy shift with 10+ patients, that's 10+ free sanity points — enough to offset multiple anomaly misses. Doctor is the best early-game sustain class and remains competitive in late-game shifts. It scales with patient throughput, so pair it with the Extra Check-In Window upgrade for double the passive procs.

Best for: Players who prioritize safety and consistency. High-volume shifts. Learning anomaly patterns without sanity pressure.

Security (1,250 Coins) — The Combat Anchor

Security starts each shift with a 5-use X-Taser, making it the best coin-class option for players who struggle with hostile encounters. Unlike the Fire Extinguisher (which you must find on the map), the X-Taser is guaranteed in your inventory from the moment the shift begins. No scrambling for weapons when a Skinwalker appears — just switch and fire. Security is particularly strong in Shifts 4-5 where the emergency rotation becomes relentless.

Best for: Hostile-heavy shifts. Players who die to Skinwalkers frequently. Solo Shift 4+ runs.

B-Tier Classes — Good in Specific Situations

Nurse (Coins) — The Best First Upgrade

Nurse is the cheapest paid class and the obvious first upgrade after Intern. It adds +3 inventory slots, giving you the breathing room to carry emergency items while still treating patients. It doesn't have the flash of Surgeon or the combat utility of Security, but for pure quality-of-life improvement per coin spent, nothing beats Nurse. Buy it as soon as you have enough coins.

Best for: Your first class purchase. Learning inventory management without the pressure.

Paramedic (Coins) — Built for Speed

Paramedic starts each shift with a Large Speed Cola (6 uses). The speed boost lets you outrun Skinwalkers in the hallways and zip between rooms during time-pressure emergencies. It's purpose-built for the Ambulance Event and any shift where the clock is your real enemy. Outside of speed-critical situations, the class doesn't offer much, but in those situations it's a lifesaver.

Best for: Ambulance events. Time-pressure shifts. Players who prefer kiting to fighting.

Secretary (Coins) — The Lobby Grinder

Secretary recovers 1 sanity per checked-in patient. Unlike Doctor (which triggers on cure) or Surgeon (which triggers on cure + speed), Secretary triggers at check-in — the first step of the workflow. This makes it the safest sanity recovery class in the game, since you get the payoff before any diagnosis, treatment, or anomaly interaction can go wrong. The tradeoff: it only works in high-volume intake shifts where patients come fast. In slow shifts, the passive barely procs.

Best for: High-intake shifts (Shift 3+). Players who want maximum safety in sanity management.

C-Tier Classes — Niche or Risky

Psychologist (Coins) — High Risk, High Reward

Psychologist doubles every sanity event — gains AND losses. It starts with 100% sanity, which sounds great until you miss an anomaly and lose 20 sanity instead of 10. This class is for skilled players only who rarely miss tells and can leverage the doubled gains for explosive snowballing. If your anomaly detection is near-perfect, Psychologist can generate more sanity than any other class. If you're still learning tells, stay far away.

Best for: Expert players with near-perfect anomaly detection. Speedrunners chasing record times.

D-Tier Classes — Replace ASAP

Intern (Free) — The Tutorial Class

Intern is the default starter class with no special abilities. Use it to learn the diagnosis flow, memorize the condition-to-item map, and build basic anomaly detection skills. The moment you have enough coins, upgrade to Nurse. Intern has no place in any run beyond your first hour of gameplay.

Best for: Literally nothing except your first 3-5 shifts while you save coins.

The Optimal Class Progression Path

If you're starting from zero and want to reach Shift 5 as efficiently as possible, here's the sequence:

  1. Shift 1-3 as Intern — Learn the basics. Save every coin. Aim for ~1,000 coins by the end of Shift 3.
  2. Buy Nurse — Your first purchase. The +3 inventory slots make every subsequent shift smoother.
  3. Grind to 2,500 coins — Run Shifts 1-3 repeatedly with Nurse. Focus on clean anomaly detection to maximize coin rewards.
  4. Buy Surgeon — Your endgame class. The sanity + speed compounding loop makes Shift 4-5 manageable.
  5. Optional: Buy Security — If you're dying to Skinwalkers in Shift 4+, Security's guaranteed X-Taser is a strong alternative while you learn combat timing.

Class Comparison: Surgeon vs Doctor

This is the most common question in the Animal Hospital Anomaly community, so let's settle it:

Factor Surgeon Doctor
Sanity per cure Yes (amount scales) 1 per cure
Speed boost on cure Yes No
Starting sanity bonus No Yes (at higher tiers)
Cost 2,500 coins Lower (coins)
Best shift range Shift 3-5+ Shift 1-4
Skill floor Medium Low

Verdict: Doctor is better for learning and early progression. Surgeon is better for endgame. If you can afford Surgeon, buy Surgeon. If you can't, Doctor will carry you comfortably through Shift 4.

Free-to-Play vs Robux Classes — What's Worth It?

If you're strictly free-to-play, your path is clear: Intern → Nurse → Surgeon. Surgeon is competitive with the Robux classes and in some ways superior — the speed boost on cure is unique and compounds in ways even Secret Agent's Gun can't replicate for certain playstyles.

If you're open to spending Robux, Head Nurse (190 Robux) is the best value. The +3 inventory slots from Shift 1 skip hours of grinding. Secret Agent (320 Robux) trivializes combat but may make the game less interesting — the tension is part of the experience. Buy it if you're stuck on Shift 5 and just want to see the ending.

Class Synergies With Shop Upgrades

To maximize your class choice, pair it with the right shop upgrades:

  • Surgeon + DNA Analysis — Faster patient IDs = more cures = more sanity + speed procs. The compound effect is insane.
  • Doctor + Extra Check-In Window — Double the patient throughput = double the passive sanity procs.
  • Security + Extra Battery Pack — Extends the X-Taser from 5 to 8 uses. Combined, you can handle every hostile in a shift.
  • Paramedic + Extra Stamina — Speed Cola already makes you fast. Stamina upgrade extends the effect duration.
  • Secretary + Faster Check-In Speed — Every speed boost at the check-in window directly feeds the passive. Maximize intake rate.

What About Multiplayer?

Class synergies matter more in multiplayer. A team with Surgeon (throughput) + Security (combat) + Doctor (sustain) covers every threat simultaneously. The Surgeon player handles patient flow while Security intercepts hostiles and Doctor patches sanity gaps. If you're coordinating with friends, diversify your class picks instead of stacking the same class.

For solo play, the emergency system is shift-based, not player-count-based — you face the same emergencies alone that a 4-player team faces together. This is why Surgeon and Secret Agent dominate solo tier lists: they give you the sustain or firepower to handle 4-player emergencies by yourself.

Pro Tip: If you're stuck in the Shift 1-3 loop, the bottleneck is usually inventory + class progression rather than skill. Stop trying to beat Shift 4 as Intern. Save coins, unlock Nurse or Surgeon, buy the DNA Analysis upgrade, then push. The game opens up dramatically once you have the right class for your playstyle.
