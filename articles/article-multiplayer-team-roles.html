Multiplayer Team Roles Guide: Best Team Compositions

How to assign roles, coordinate schedules, and build the perfect co-op team for every shift in Animal Hospital (Anomaly).

Multiplayer Guide
July 8, 2026 | 12 min read

Animal Hospital (Anomaly) supports 1–4 player co-op, and the difference between a coordinated team and a group of randoms is night and day. Solo play is already demanding — but with the right team composition, you can cover every anomaly check, handle simultaneous emergencies, and farm coins far more efficiently than any lone doctor could.

This guide breaks down the best multiplayer team roles, recommended class pairings, communication strategies, and shift-by-shift coordination tips. Whether you're running a casual 2-player session or a full 4-player speed-clear, this is your definitive co-op playbook.

Why Multiplayer Changes Everything

In solo play, you're responsible for every role simultaneously — anomaly detection, patient treatment, emergency response, and sanity management. In multiplayer, you can split these responsibilities across dedicated roles. This means:

  • Faster anomaly checks — one player handles visual/photo while another checks CCTV/paperwork
  • Instant emergency response — a dedicated combat player can intercept Skinwalkers while medics continue treatment
  • Parallel patient processing — multiple patients can be checked in and treated at the same time
  • Reduced individual sanity drain — shared workload means less pressure per player

The Four Core Team Roles

Every effective team — regardless of size — needs to cover these four functions. In smaller teams, one player may fill multiple roles.

Role 1: The Gatekeeper (Anomaly Detector)

The Gatekeeper sits at the intake window and performs all four anomaly checks on every patient before admitting them. This is the most critical role because every missed anomaly spawns a Skinwalker. The Gatekeeper needs:

  • Sharp eyesight — fast visual anomaly recognition
  • Muscle memory — photo, CCTV, and paperwork check routine
  • Patience — never rush a check, even under pressure

Best classes: Doctor (balanced stats) or Nurse (faster treatment speed for post-admission care).

Role 2: The Medic (Treatment Specialist)

The Medic handles all patient treatment once patients are admitted. They move between rooms, administer items, and complete treatment minigames. Key responsibilities:

  • Know the treatment workflow for every condition
  • Carry the right treatment items for the current shift's patient pool
  • Manage room assignments and treatment priority

Best class: Surgeon (6 inventory slots, fastest treatment speed, best for Shift 4–5).

Role 3: The Enforcer (Combat & Emergency)

The Enforcer is the team's security force. They patrol the hospital floor, eliminate Skinwalkers, respond to emergency events, and protect other players. This role requires:

  • Always carrying a weapon (Taser, Gun, or Fire Extinguisher)
  • Positioning near high-traffic areas (hallway intersections, Room 8)
  • Reacting within seconds to emergency alerts

Best class: Security (starts with X-Taser, 5 uses, bonus movement speed).

Role 4: The Runner (Utility & Support)

The Runner handles everything that falls between the cracks — fetching items, managing CCTV monitors, handling paperwork anomalies, and providing backup during emergencies. Think of them as the team's flex player:

  • Stockpile items from the shop and distribute to teammates
  • Monitor CCTV feeds for CCTV anomalies
  • Provide sanity recovery items (Coffee, Chocolate) to teammates in need
  • Backup the Enforcer during multi-event cascades

Best class: Intern (3 slots but fastest movement speed) or Nurse (balanced support stats).

2-Player Team Strategy

Two-player is the most popular co-op format and strikes the best balance between challenge and efficiency. Here's the detailed breakdown:

Player 1: Gatekeeper-Medic (The Frontline)

Player 1 stays near the intake window and treatment rooms. Their workflow:

  1. Check in every patient — visual, photo, CCTV, paperwork (all four layers)
  2. Admit clean patients and assign them to rooms
  3. Treat patients using the treatment workflow
  4. Call out anomalies to Player 2 via voice chat

Recommended class: Surgeon — 6 inventory slots let you carry treatment items AND sanity recovery.

Player 2: Enforcer-Runner (The Floor)

Player 2 patrols the hospital floor and handles all threats. Their workflow:

  1. Patrol hallways for Skinwalkers and anomalies
  2. Eliminate threats with Taser or Fire Extinguisher
  3. Respond to emergency events (fire, Surgery Monster, Death Ritual)
  4. Fetch items from the shop and deliver to Player 1
  5. Monitor CCTV when Player 1 is busy with treatment

Recommended class: Security — X-Taser starting weapon + movement speed bonus.

Pro Tip: Voice Chat Is Mandatory

2-player co-op without voice communication is significantly harder. Use Discord, in-game voice, or any real-time channel. Text chat is too slow when a Skinwalker grabs your teammate. Quick callouts like "Skinwalker left hall," "Fire in Room 3," or "Need Coffee at intake" save runs.

3-Player Team Strategy

Three players unlocks the sweet spot — you can have a dedicated Gatekeeper who focuses solely on anomaly detection. This dramatically reduces Skinwalker spawns because the Gatekeeper never gets distracted by treatment or combat.

Positioning

  • Player 1 (Gatekeeper — Doctor): Stays at intake. Never leaves the window unless the shift is clear.
  • Player 2 (Medic — Surgeon): Moves between treatment rooms. Communicates item needs to Player 3.
  • Player 3 (Enforcer-Runner — Security): Patrols the floor, handles all emergencies, runs shop errands.

Communication Protocol

With three players, establish a clear callout system:

  • "Clean" — patient passed all checks, safe to admit
  • "Anomaly [type]" — spotted a specific anomaly, reject the patient
  • "Emergency [event]" — active event requiring Enforcer response
  • "Need [item] at [location]" — item request from Medic to Runner
  • "Skinwalker [location]" — hostile spotted, Enforcer responds

4-Player Team Strategy

Four-player teams can dominate every shift with proper coordination. Each player has a single dedicated role, which means zero overlap and maximum efficiency.

The Dream Team

  • Player 1 — Gatekeeper (Doctor): Pure anomaly detection. Checks every patient with zero distractions.
  • Player 2 — Medic (Surgeon): Pure treatment. Knows every condition-to-item mapping.
  • Player 3 — Enforcer (Security): Pure combat. Carries Taser + Fire Extinguisher. Responds to all threats.
  • Player 4 — Runner (Nurse): Pure support. Shops, distributes items, monitors CCTV, handles paperwork anomalies.

Shift 5 with 4 Players

Shift 5 is brutal solo but manageable with a full team. The key is emergency cascade management — when multiple events fire simultaneously (which happens often in Shift 5), the team splits:

  • Enforcer handles the first emergency
  • Runner handles the second
  • Gatekeeper keeps checking patients (never stop — queue builds fast)
  • Medic keeps treating (patient timers don't pause for emergencies)

Common Multiplayer Mistakes

Avoid these team-killing errors:

  • Everyone chasing the same Skinwalker — designate ONE Enforcer. Others keep working.
  • Skipping anomaly checks because "someone else will catch it" — the Gatekeeper must check EVERY patient, no exceptions.
  • Hogging items — distribute sanity items evenly. One player going insane hurts the whole team.
  • No callouts — silent teams die. Even basic pings are better than nothing.
  • Ignoring the queue — in multiplayer, the patient queue fills faster. Keep processing or the timer pressure will overwhelm you.
  • All four players in the same room — spread out. Cover different wings of the hospital.

Sanity Management in Co-Op

Sanity works differently in multiplayer. Each player has their own sanity bar, but the team shares the consequences:

  • If ANY player hits zero sanity, they become a liability — hallucinations, impaired vision, slower movement
  • Low-sanity players may see false anomalies, causing them to reject clean patients (wasting time) or admit dirty ones (spawning Skinwalkers)
  • The Runner should carry extra Coffee and Chocolate and distribute them proactively

Rule of thumb: if a teammate asks for Coffee, give it immediately. A player at low sanity is more dangerous to the team than a single Skinwalker.

Multiplayer Coin Farming

Co-op is the most efficient way to farm coins in Animal Hospital Anomaly. With proper role assignment:

  • Patients are processed faster → more patients treated per shift → more coins
  • Fewer Skinwalkers spawn (anomaly checks are thorough) → less time wasted on combat
  • Emergencies are handled faster → less sanity drain → fewer item purchases needed

A coordinated 4-player team on Shift 3 can earn significantly more coins per hour than solo Shift 5 runs. For detailed coin strategies, see our Coin Farming Guide.

Multiplayer in Animal Hospital Anomaly isn't just "solo with more people" — it's a fundamentally different game. Assign roles, communicate constantly, and trust your teammates to handle their responsibilities. The hospital runs best when every doctor knows their job.
