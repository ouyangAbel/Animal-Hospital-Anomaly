Anomaly Priority Guide: Which Anomalies Are Most Dangerous

Risk-ranked tier list of every anomaly in Animal Hospital Anomaly — from lethal Skinwalker converters to harmless visual quirks. Learn what to reject first and what can wait.

Strategy Guide
July 25, 2026 | 16 min read

Not all anomalies in Animal Hospital Anomaly are created equal. Some guarantee a Skinwalker if admitted. Others trigger instant emergencies. And a few are so subtle that even experienced players miss them under pressure. This guide ranks every anomaly by threat level — the actual danger it poses if it slips past your detection and gets admitted.

For the complete detection workflow, see our Anomaly Detection Flowchart. For individual anomaly tells, visit the Character Guides.

The Threat Level System

Every anomaly is ranked on a 5-tier threat scale based on three factors: Skinwalker conversion risk, emergency trigger severity, and detection difficulty. An anomaly that converts to a Skinwalker AND is hard to detect scores higher than one that's obvious but harmless.

Tier Definitions

  • S-Tier — Lethal: Guaranteed Skinwalker conversion or instant emergency. Missing these ends runs.
  • A-Tier — Critical: High Skinwalker conversion risk or severe emergency. Requires immediate rejection.
  • B-Tier — Dangerous: Moderate conversion risk. Can cascade into emergencies if combined with other failures.
  • C-Tier — Moderate: Low conversion risk but easy to miss. Accumulate over time and cause problems.
  • D-Tier — Low: Minimal threat. Harmless if admitted, but still worth rejecting for score.

S-Tier Anomalies: The Run-Enders

These three anomalies are the most dangerous in the game. Missing any one of them can end your run immediately or within seconds.

Hollow Face — The Death Ritual Trigger

The Hollow Face is the single most dangerous anomaly because it triggers a Death Ritual on admission — a levitating patient surrounded by candles with a 30-60 second timer. If you don't cancel it with a Taser, Defibrillator, or Eye Drops, the patient dies and converts to a Skinwalker. That's a two-for-one failure: lost patient plus hostile spawn.

Detection: Dark hollow eyes, twitching motions, hunched posture. Visible on both visual check and photo. Always reject at the window.

Skinwalker — The Instant Hostile

The Skinwalker anomaly is what happens when you admit a patient who is already a Skinwalker in disguise. It immediately drops the disguise and attacks. No timer, no warning — just instant combat. This is why the visual check at the window is your most important layer.

Detection: Unnatural movements, distorted face, wrong proportions. Visible on visual check and CCTV. Never admit under any circumstances.

Surgery Monster — The 45-Second Crisis

The Surgery Monster anomaly triggers when you attempt Room 8 surgery on an infected patient. Purple tentacles emerge and you have exactly 45 seconds to complete the surgery minigame. Fail and the patient dies. This is the tightest timer in the game.

Detection: Visible on visual check and paperwork. Always cross-reference the appointment list before entering Room 8.

A-Tier Anomalies: High Priority Threats

A-Tier anomalies convert to Skinwalkers if admitted but don't trigger instant emergencies. They're dangerous because Skinwalkers are the primary run-killer in the game.

The Photo-Class Trio: Three Eyes, Wide Eyes Smile, Mass of Eyes

These three anomalies are all detectable on photo check and all convert to Skinwalkers if admitted. They're grouped together because they share the same detection layer and consequence. The photo check is the most commonly skipped layer — don't skip it.

Strategy: Always snap a photo. Compare the photo to the patient. If anything looks wrong — extra eyes, wrong smile, too many eyes — reject immediately.

Bed Monster — The Silent Spawn

The Bed Monster anomaly doesn't convert to a Skinwalker. Instead, it spawns a Bed Monster under the patient's bed after admission. If you don't apply Maple Syrup within 30 seconds, it triggers the Monster Eating Patient emergency. The counter is simple (Maple Syrup) but the timer is tight.

Strategy: Check under every bed after admitting a patient. Carry Maple Syrup as insurance.

B-Tier Anomalies: Moderate Threats

B-Tier anomalies convert to Skinwalkers but are easier to detect or less immediately dangerous. They're still worth rejecting but won't end your run on their own.

CCTV-Only Anomalies: Stalker & Camera Figure

The Stalker and Camera Figure anomalies are only visible on CCTV. They don't show up on visual check or photo. This makes them particularly dangerous because many players skip the CCTV check when busy.

Strategy: Make CCTV check a non-negotiable part of your workflow. Every patient, every time. These anomalies are the reason CCTV exists.

Visual + Photo Anomalies: Unnatural Body, Human Teeth, Different Eyes, Different Ears

These anomalies are visible on both visual check and photo. They're moderate threat because they require two layers to both fail — but under pressure, players often rush the visual check and skip the photo.

Strategy: Use the photo check as your safety net. If you rushed the visual check, the photo will catch what you missed.

C-Tier Anomalies: Low Priority But Easy to Miss

C-Tier anomalies cause minor sanity drain or wrong treatment but don't convert to Skinwalkers. They're dangerous in aggregate — admit enough of them and your sanity will crater.

Photo-Only Anomalies: Censored Eyes, Cursed Photo, Incorrect Photo, Static Photo, Unnatural Photo

These five anomalies are only visible on photo check. They cause minor sanity drain if admitted but no Skinwalker conversion. The danger is that they're easy to miss if you skip the photo check.

Strategy: Always snap a photo. Even if you're confident in your visual check, the photo is your backup layer.

Paperwork-Only Anomalies: Mimic Patient & Missing Appointment

The Mimic Patient and Missing Appointment anomalies are only visible on the appointment list. Admitting them doesn't spawn a Skinwalker — instead, you'll give the wrong treatment to the wrong patient, wasting time and resources.

Strategy: Cross-reference every patient against the appointment list before administering treatment.

D-Tier Anomalies: Harmless But Worth Rejecting

D-Tier anomalies are visible on visual check and are completely harmless if admitted. They don't convert to Skinwalkers, don't trigger emergencies, and don't drain sanity. Reject them for score points, but don't panic if one slips through.

The Harmless Five: Slime, Ghost, Hiders, Head Banger, Void Body

These five anomalies are cosmetic quirks that pose zero threat. The Slime anomaly makes the patient look slimy. The Ghost anomaly gives them a translucent appearance. Hiders partially hide their face. Head Banger has an enlarged head. Void Body has a dark void texture.

Strategy: Reject for score, but if you miss one, don't stress. Focus your attention on S and A-Tier anomalies first.

Priority Workflow: What to Check First

When you're overwhelmed — multiple patients waiting, timers firing, sanity dropping — you need a priority order. Here's the optimal workflow:

Step 1: Visual Check at the Window (2 seconds)

This catches S-Tier and most A-Tier anomalies. Look for Hollow Face, Skinwalker, Three Eyes, Wide Eyes Smile, Mass of Body. If you see anything wrong, reject immediately. No need to continue checking.

Step 2: Photo Check (1 second)

Snap a photo. Compare. This catches photo-class anomalies that the visual check missed. If the photo looks wrong, reject.

Step 3: CCTV Check (2 seconds)

Open CCTV. Look for Stalker, Camera Figure, Twitching, Bed Monster. If anything moves on CCTV that shouldn't, reject.

Step 4: Paperwork Check (1 second)

Cross-reference the appointment list. Look for Mimic Patient, Missing Appointment, Surgery Monster. If the name doesn't match, reject.

Total Time: 6 Seconds Per Patient

The full four-layer check takes approximately 6 seconds. That's faster than dealing with any emergency. Make it a reflex.

Shift-Specific Priority Adjustments

Not all shifts have the same anomaly distribution. Adjust your priorities based on the shift you're playing.

Shift 1-2: Focus on Visual + Photo

Early shifts have fewer anomalies and they're mostly visual/photo class. Master the visual check and photo check first. CCTV and paperwork anomalies are rare in early shifts.

Shift 3: All Layers Active

Shift 3 is where all four detection layers become critical. CCTV-only anomalies (Stalker, Camera Figure) start appearing. Paperwork anomalies become more common. Run the full four-layer check on every patient.

Shift 4-5: Speed + Accuracy

Late shifts have more patients and more anomalies. You'll be tempted to skip layers. Don't. The time you "save" by skipping CCTV is lost tenfold when a Stalker converts to a Skinwalker.

Pro Tip: The 80/20 Rule

80% of run-ending failures come from just 5 anomalies: Hollow Face, Skinwalker, Surgery Monster, Three Eyes, and Bed Monster. If you can perfectly detect these five, you'll survive 80% of shifts. Master the S-Tier and A-Tier before worrying about C-Tier and D-Tier.

Common Mistakes by Threat Level

Players make different mistakes depending on the threat level. Here's what to watch for:

S-Tier Mistakes

  • Rushing the visual check — spending less than 1 second looking at the patient
  • Admitting "just to check" — thinking you can handle the emergency if it triggers
  • Ignoring Hollow Face — not recognizing the dark hollow eyes tell

A-Tier Mistakes

  • Skipping the photo check — the most commonly skipped layer
  • Forgetting to check under beds — Bed Monster spawns silently
  • Not carrying Maple Syrup — no counter for Bed Monster if you don't have it

B-Tier Mistakes

  • Skipping CCTV — "I'll check it later" means you'll forget
  • Not cross-referencing paperwork — Mimic Patient wastes treatment items
  • Assuming visual check is enough — CCTV-only anomalies exist specifically to punish this

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the most dangerous anomaly in Animal Hospital Anomaly?

The Hollow Face is the most dangerous anomaly because it triggers a guaranteed Death Ritual on admission, which if not cancelled within 30-60 seconds, kills the patient AND converts them to a Skinwalker. That's a two-for-one failure. The Skinwalker anomaly itself is equally dangerous because it drops its disguise and attacks immediately with no timer window to react.

Which anomalies should I reject first when overwhelmed?

Prioritize S-Tier anomalies first: Hollow Face, Skinwalker, and Surgery Monster. These are the only anomalies that can end your run instantly. Next, watch for A-Tier: Three Eyes, Wide Eyes Smile, Mass of Eyes, and Bed Monster. If you're overwhelmed, skip C-Tier and D-Tier — they cause minor sanity drain but won't end your run.

Do D-Tier anomalies matter if I accidentally admit them?

No. D-Tier anomalies (Slime, Ghost, Hiders, Head Banger, Void Body) are completely harmless if admitted. They don't convert to Skinwalkers, don't trigger emergencies, and don't drain sanity. Reject them for score points, but don't panic if one slips through. Focus your attention on S-Tier and A-Tier anomalies instead.

Why are CCTV-only anomalies considered B-Tier instead of A-Tier?

CCTV-only anomalies like Stalker and Camera Figure are B-Tier because they convert to Skinwalkers (same as A-Tier) but are impossible to detect without CCTV. This makes them more about workflow discipline than reaction speed. If you run the full four-layer check on every patient, CCTV-only anomalies are caught every time. They're only dangerous if you skip the CCTV layer.

How many anomalies are there in Animal Hospital Anomaly?

There are 28 anomalies in Animal Hospital Anomaly, spread across four detection layers: Visual (14 anomalies), Photo (10 anomalies), CCTV (6 anomalies), and Paperwork (3 anomalies). Some anomalies appear on multiple layers. The complete list with individual tell descriptions is available on our Character Guides page.

Can I survive admitting a Surgery Monster anomaly?

Yes, but it's extremely risky. The Surgery Monster triggers a 45-second timer when you enter Room 8. You must complete the surgery minigame before the timer expires. The tool layout is randomised each shift, so muscle memory won't help. If you have full health, full sanity, and know the required treatment, you can survive. But if any other emergency fires during surgery (fire event, Bed Monster), you're trapped in Room 8 with no exit. Prevention is always better than cure.
