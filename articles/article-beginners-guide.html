Animal Hospital Anomaly Beginner's Guide: Complete Walkthrough for New Players (2026)

From your first click to surviving Shift 3 with confidence — hospital layout, treatment workflow, anomaly detection, sanity, emergencies, coins, and everything a new player needs.

Beginner Guide
July 2, 2026 | 13 min read

You just launched Animal Hospital (Anomaly) and the first thing you see is a dim hallway, a lobby counter, and eight patient rooms. No tutorial. No minimap. No hand-holding. The game drops you into the deep end with nothing but an Intern badge and three inventory slots. If that sounds overwhelming, you're in the right place.

This Animal Hospital Anomaly beginner's guide is the walkthrough the game doesn't give you. We cover the hospital layout, exactly how to treat your first patient, the three anomaly detection layers you'll use every single shift, how sanity actually works, what those blaring alarms mean, and the coin economy that unlocks every class and upgrade. By the end, you'll walk into Shift 1 knowing what to do — and more importantly, what NOT to do.

Getting Started: What Actually Happens When You Load In

Your first session should be spent in Shift 1 with the Intern class. Intern has no special abilities and only 3 inventory slots — and that's exactly the point. Shift 1 exists to teach you the fundamentals without emergencies or hostile enemies complicating things. Do not try to skip ahead. Do not buy items you don't understand. Run Shift 1 three to five times until the treatment workflow feels automatic.

When you load into the hospital, take 10 seconds to orient yourself. The Lobby (where patients check in) is the central hub. From the lobby, hallways branch to 8 patient rooms (numbered 1 through 8), the Pharmacy (where treatment items are picked up), the CCTV room (monitor hallway cameras), and the Supply Closet (emergency items spawn here). Run a lap. Touch every room. Knowing the layout cold saves you the 5-10 seconds of fumbling that kill runs later.

Your First Patient: The 6-Step Treatment Flow

Every patient in Animal Hospital Anomaly follows the same chain. Master it once and you've mastered it forever:

  1. Check-In (Lobby) — A patient arrives at the front desk. Interact with the check-in window to register them. This assigns them a room number and generates their paperwork.
  2. Diagnosis (Patient Room) — Go to the assigned room. Look at the patient. Read the chart on the wall. The chart tells you the condition (e.g., "Broken Leg," "Fever," "Cough").
  3. Item Match (Pharmacy) — Walk to the Pharmacy and pick up the item that matches the condition. Broken Leg = Splint. Fever = Ice Pack. Cough = Cough Syrup. Every condition has exactly one correct item — there is no ambiguity.
  4. Treat (Patient Room) — Return to the patient, equip the item from your inventory, and use it on them. If the item is correct, a green success indicator appears.
  5. Anomaly Check — Before you discharge the patient, check ALL three detection layers: visual inspection of the patient, CCTV review of their hallway behavior, and paperwork verification (chart matches photo, appointment exists). This is the step that separates survivors from casualties.
  6. Discharge (Lobby) — Return to the lobby and discharge the patient at the counter. You get paid in coins. The bed frees up for the next patient.

The most common beginner mistake is skipping step 5. Players get excited about a successful treatment, rush to discharge, and lose 10-15 sanity because the patient had three eyes and they never looked. Slow down. Check everything.

The Three Anomaly Detection Layers — Never Skip Any

Anomaly detection in Animal Hospital Anomaly operates across three independent layers. A patient can be clean on two layers and compromised on the third. You must check all three every single time:

Layer 1: Visual Anomalies

Look directly at the patient model. Check the face, eyes, mouth, ears, and body for anything that shouldn't be there. Common visual anomalies include three eyes, hollow face, mismatched eyes, sharp teeth, void body (completely black texture), and twitching. Get close. Rotate your camera. These are the easiest anomalies to spot and the most embarrassing to miss. See our Character Guides for the full anomaly catalog with images.

Layer 2: CCTV Anomalies

Visit the CCTV room and cycle through the hallway cameras. Watch for patients who stare directly at the camera, move when they shouldn't, or exhibit any behavior that doesn't match the other patients. CCTV anomalies are subtle — a patient might look normal in their room but stare into the lens the moment they're in the hallway. Check CCTV after treatment but before discharge, since patients walk back to the lobby through the halls.

Layer 3: Paperwork Anomalies

Read the patient's chart and appointment record. Look for incorrect photos (photo doesn't match the patient model), static/distorted photos, missing appointments (patient has no appointment entry at all), or cursed photos (image is visibly corrupted). Paperwork anomalies are the most overlooked by beginners because reading charts feels tedious — but they're also the most consistent. A patient's chart doesn't change mid-shift. Get in the habit of glancing at the photo every time.

Sanity 101: The One Stat That Ends Your Run

Sanity starts at 100% and is displayed prominently on your HUD. Every missed anomaly costs 10-15 sanity. Every hostile encounter that damages you costs additional sanity. When sanity hits 0%, the shift ends immediately — game over, no coins, no progress.

Sanity does NOT passively regenerate. You must actively restore it using consumable items: Chocolate (+20 sanity, slow eat), Coffee (+15 sanity, fast drink), Eye Drops (+10 sanity, instant), or Soda (+25 sanity but rare). Keep at least one sanity restoration item in your inventory at all times. The moment you drop below 50%, use it — do not wait. A surprise anomaly chain in the late shift can drop you from 50% to 0% faster than you can run to the Pharmacy.

Emergency Events: The Alarms That Mean Business

Starting from Shift 3, the game introduces emergency events — scripted, timed scenarios that interrupt the normal patient flow. When an emergency triggers, you'll hear an alarm and see an on-screen timer. You have 30-120 seconds (depending on the event) to respond correctly or face severe consequences — lost sanity, dead patients, or shift failure.

The eight emergency events in Animal Hospital Anomaly are:

  • Ambulance Surge — 6+ patients arrive simultaneously. Triage and treat fast.
  • Death Ritual — A patient performs a ritual. Counter with Taser, Defibrillator, Eye Drops, or kicking candles.
  • Surgery Monster — Room 8 tendril encounter. Complete the surgery minigame within 45 seconds.
  • Patient on Fire — Extinguish with Fire Extinguisher or Ointment within 60 seconds.
  • Critical Patient — Full-chain treatment with a 100-120 second timer.
  • Monster Eating Patient — Counter with Maple Syrup within 30 seconds.
  • Fire in Room — Extinguish room fire; reveals Skinwalker presence.
  • Patient Fainted — Carry to bed or use Smelling Salts within 60 seconds.

Beginners should never attempt Shift 3 without at least one emergency counter-item in their inventory. Our Boss Guides page has complete walkthroughs for every emergency.

Inventory Management: The 3-Slot Puzzle

As an Intern, you have exactly 3 inventory slots. Every item you carry competes for those slots, and you can't pause to reorganize during an emergency. Here's the optimal beginner loadout for Shift 1-2:

  • Slot 1: Treatment item (whatever your current patient needs)
  • Slot 2: Sanity restoration item (Chocolate or Coffee)
  • Slot 3: Empty — use for the NEXT patient's treatment item you grab proactively

Once you unlock the Nurse class (+3 slots, total 6) or buy the Extra Inventory Pouch shop upgrade, you can carry emergency counter-items alongside treatment and sanity items. Until then, the 3-slot limitation is your primary constraint — plan every Pharmacy trip with intention. Read the Equipment & Build Guides for full item stats and loadout strategies.

The Coin Economy: Earn, Save, Spend Smart

Coins are earned at the end of every completed shift. The base reward increases with shift number and is multiplied by clean anomaly detection (fewer misses = more coins). A clean Shift 1 run with zero missed anomalies might earn ~200-300 coins; Shift 3 with perfect detection can earn 500+.

The single best use of your first coins is unlocking the Nurse class. Nurse costs significantly less than Surgeon (2,500 coins) and the +3 inventory slots immediately make every subsequent shift easier. After Nurse, save for the DNA Analysis shop upgrade, which accelerates patient identification. Only then should you consider Surgeon — the best free class in the game but priced at 2,500 coins.

  • First 1,000 coins: Buy Nurse (+3 inventory slots)
  • Next 1,500 coins: Buy DNA Analysis upgrade (faster diagnosis)
  • Next 2,500 coins: Buy Surgeon (sanity + speed on every cure)
  • Everything after: Extra Inventory Pouch, Extra Check-In Window, Extra Battery Pack

Common Beginner Mistakes (and How to Avoid Them)

Nearly every new player makes these mistakes. Learn from them now instead of during a live shift:

  1. Rushing to discharge without anomaly checks. Visual, CCTV, paperwork — check all three every time. The 10 seconds you save by skipping is never worth 15 sanity.
  2. Running out of sanity items. The moment your sanity drops below 50%, use a restoration item. Do not gamble that "one more patient" won't be anomalous.
  3. Treating the wrong patient. When you have multiple patients, match the treatment item to the correct room number. Mixing up rooms is a waste of time and items.
  4. Ignoring CCTV entirely. CCTV is not optional. Some anomalies ONLY appear on CCTV and are invisible at the patient's bedside.
  5. Buying random items before understanding the shop. Every coin spent on items you don't need is a coin you can't spend on Nurse or DNA Analysis. Prioritize permanent upgrades.
  6. Jumping to Shift 3 too early. Shift 3 introduces emergencies. If you can't consistently clear Shift 1-2 with zero anomaly misses, you are not ready for emergencies.

When Are You Ready for Shift 2?

You are ready to progress from Shift 1 to Shift 2 when ALL of the following are true:

  • You can treat 5+ patients in a shift without looking at a condition-to-item reference
  • You check all three anomaly detection layers on every patient by reflex
  • You have not dropped below 40% sanity in your last two Shift 1 runs
  • You've saved at least 500 coins

If you meet those four conditions, move to Shift 2. If not, run Shift 1 again. There is no penalty for repeating early shifts — the coins add up and the muscle memory compounds.

The First 5 Hours: A Recommended Progression Timeline

  • Hour 1: Run Shift 1 × 5 as Intern. Learn the layout, memorize the 6-step workflow, build anomaly-check reflex. Save all coins.
  • Hour 2: Buy Nurse. Run Shift 1-2 with 6 inventory slots. Practice CCTV and paperwork checks. Start recognizing common visual anomalies by sight.
  • Hour 3: Buy DNA Analysis upgrade. Push to Shift 3. Experience your first emergency events.
  • Hour 4: Grind Shift 2-3 to save 2,500 coins. Study the emergency event counters and the anomaly catalog.
  • Hour 5: Buy Surgeon. The game changes. Sanity + speed on every cure means you can handle the Shift 4-5 chaos.
New Player Tip: Shift 1 exists to be repeated. The game's learning curve is steep by design — it filters out players who skip fundamentals. Every clean Shift 1 run earns you coins AND builds the muscle memory that makes emergencies manageable later. Marathon runners don't skip to mile 20 on day one. Run your laps.
