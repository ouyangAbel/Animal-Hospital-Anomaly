Stalker

How to counter the Stalker enemy in Animal Hospital Anomaly. Rat figure, sanity cost, and eye contact avoidance.

Passive
July 1, 2026

The Stalker is a passive enemy in Animal Hospital Anomaly. A once-per-run rat figure that lurks at corners. Looking directly at it costs 10 sanity, then it vanishes. This guide covers how to detect Stalker, the sanity cost, and how to avoid eye contact.

What Is the Stalker?

The Stalker is a rat-like figure that appears once per run, lurking at corners in the hospital. It doesn't chase you or deal direct damage, but looking directly at it costs 10 sanity. After you look at it, the Stalker vanishes and doesn't appear again for the rest of the run.

How to Detect Stalker

  • Look for the rat figure -- A small, rat-like silhouette lurking at a corner.
  • Note the location -- The Stalker appears at corners, often in hallways or room entrances.
  • Avoid direct eye contact -- Looking at it costs 10 sanity.

The Sanity Cost

Looking directly at the Stalker costs 10 sanity. This is a significant amount, especially in higher shifts when sanity management becomes critical. The Stalker vanishes after you look at it, so you only pay the cost once per run.

Counter-Strategy: Avoid Eye Contact

The best counter is avoiding direct eye contact. When you encounter Stalker:

  1. Keep your camera angled toward the floor
  2. Avoid looking directly at corners where the Stalker might be
  3. If you see the Stalker, look away immediately
  4. Continue with your normal workflow without engaging

Why Avoidance Is Better

The Stalker doesn't chase you or deal direct damage. It only costs sanity if you look at it. By keeping your camera angled down and avoiding corners, you can complete the run without ever paying the 10 sanity cost.

Shift Appearances

The Stalker appears starting in Shift 7 and continues through higher shifts. It's a late-game enemy that tests your spatial awareness.
