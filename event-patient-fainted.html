Patient Fainted Emergency Guide: 60-Second Carry Counter in Animal Hospital Anomaly

Carry fainted patients to the designated bed within 60 seconds, or use Smelling Salts for the instant recovery.

Emergency Guide
2026-07-01 | 5 min read

The Patient Fainted emergency is unique because it requires a physical carry to resolve. A patient collapses and you must transport them from wherever they fell to a designated recovery bed within 60 seconds. It is not the hardest emergency, but during an Ambulance cascade, carrying a patient while other timers are ticking is where runs fall apart.

Trigger Conditions

Patients can faint from check-in stress, fire contact, gunshot proximity, or extreme sanity shock events. Firing the Gun near patients causes them to faint from the noise shock — never shoot near the intake area.

The 60-Second Timer and Carry Mechanic

From the moment a patient collapses, you have 60 seconds to pick them up and place them on a designated recovery bed. The timer stops the moment the patient is placed. While carrying, you cannot use any other items or weapons. Movement speed is reduced. If the timer expires, the patient dies.

Counter Options

  1. Smelling Salts (fast counter) — Apply to the fainted patient for instant recovery on the spot. No carry required. Preferred method.
  2. Manual carry (standard counter) — Pick up and transport to the designated bed. Takes 5-15 seconds. Always the fallback option.

Cascade Risk During Ambulance Events

During Ambulance Events, multiple patients can faint simultaneously from fire/sanity shock. Carrying a patient makes you unable to counter other active emergencies. Always prioritise active damage (fire) over timed transport (carry). Smelling Salts are relatively affordable ($100-$150) — carry at least one from Shift 3 onwards to turn a 60-second transport into a 2-second instant fix.
