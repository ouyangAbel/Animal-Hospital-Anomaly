Critical Patient Emergency Guide: Full Treatment Chain in Animal Hospital Anomaly

Master the Critical Patient emergency — the only timed event that requires the full treatment chain under pressure.

Emergency Guide
2026-07-01 | 7 min read

The Critical Patient emergency is widely considered the hardest timed crisis in Animal Hospital (Anomaly). Unlike other emergencies that require a single action, the Critical Patient needs the entire treatment chain: diagnose, procure, administer, monitor. And you have 100-120 seconds to do it while other things are likely on fire.

How It Triggers

Critical Patients appear during ambulance waves starting Shift 4. Each ambulance wave can bring one or more Critical Patients, each with their own independent per-arrival timer. The timer starts the moment the patient enters the hospital — not when you first interact with them.

The 100-120 Second Timer

The timer varies: most Critical Patients have 100-120 seconds, but some can have as little as 60 seconds. If the timer expires, the patient dies. There is no partial credit — the full treatment chain must complete before the timer hits zero.

The Full Treatment Chain

  1. Diagnose — Examine the patient and identify the condition. This step takes 3-5 seconds if you know what to look for.
  2. Procure — Go to the appropriate supply room and fetch the treatment item. Room location matters — know your hospital layout.
  3. Administer — Apply the treatment to the patient. Antibiotics for Infection, Bandages for Injury, Cold Pack for Fever, etc. Using the wrong item wastes time.
  4. Monitor — Wait for the treatment to take effect. The patient must stabilise before the timer expires.

Why Critical Patient Is the Hardest Emergency

Other emergencies need one action. Patient on Fire? Spray extinguisher. Death Ritual? Tase. But the Critical Patient requires four sequential steps that cannot be skipped or accelerated (other than movement speed). This means you are locked into a multi-step process while other emergencies may be firing simultaneously. Preparation is everything: know the hospital layout, pre-stock common treatment items, and triage before the timer starts.
