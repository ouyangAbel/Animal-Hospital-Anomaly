Animal Hospital Anomaly Events & Emergencies Guide

Timed emergencies that can interrupt your shift 锟?what triggers them, the clock you're racing, and how to resolve before the consequence lands.

Emergencies start at Shift 3. Before that, your shifts are mostly intake + treatment loops. Once Shift 3 hits, expect interruptions at random 锟?and every emergency has its own clock. Knowing the timer for each one is the difference between handling and panicking.

There are 6 timed emergencies and 2 event types in the game. This guide covers every one with verified timers and counter-actions.

Emergency Timer Reference

Six Timed Emergencies

  • Patient on Fire 锟?60 seconds. Counter: Fire Extinguisher (fastest), or manual stop + Ointment.
  • Patient Fainted 锟?60 seconds. Counter: Carry to designated bed (timer stops on placement).
  • Death Ritual 锟?30-60 seconds. Counter: Extinguish all candles OR defibrillate patient.
  • Critical Patient 锟?100-120 seconds. Counter: Full treatment chain 锟?bed alone isn't enough.
  • Surgery Monster (Tendril) 锟?45 seconds. Counter: Complete the Room 8 surgery minigame.
  • Monster Eating Patient 锟?30 seconds. Counter: Maple Syrup to the Bed Monster site.

Detailed Emergency Guides

Ambulance Event

6+ patients arrive simultaneously starting Shift 4. Dr. Harlow announces an anomaly attack. Recurring every 5 shifts. Bonus cash on successful completion. The chaos is real 锟?multiple emergencies can stack on top of each other within the surge.

Event

Patient on Fire

Burning patient sprints through the hospital 锟?60 seconds to extinguish. Fire Extinguisher is the one-shot fix; Ointment is the manual fallback. Can potentially be an anomaly. Drains -1 sanity/second while burning.

60s Timer

Death Ritual

Patient levitates surrounded by ritual candles. Multiple cancel paths: kick candles (1-3 sanity each), tase patient (no sanity loss), or apply Eye Drops/IV Drops/Coffee. Timer varies by source (30-60s) 锟?treat 30s as the safe planning number.

30-60s Timer

Critical Patient

Per-arrival timer attached to one patient during ambulance waves 锟?100-120 seconds to bed and stabilise. Sometimes 60s. Triggers every 4 shifts, common during Ambulance Events. The hardest emergency because it requires the full treatment chain.

100-120s Timer

Monster Emerging (Surgery)

Purple monster emerges during patient recovery in Room 8 锟?45 seconds. Complete the surgery mini-game to save the patient. Tasing or shooting kills the patient and requires restart. The tightest timer in the game.

45s Timer

Monster Eating Patient

Bed Monster grabbed a patient 锟?30 seconds before the patient is consumed. Maple Syrup is the documented counter. The shortest timer in the game 锟?react immediately.

30s Timer

Fire in Room

Treatment room ignites 锟?drains -1 sanity/second while burning. New patients entering faint immediately; patients entering WITHOUT fainting reveal as Skinwalkers. Fire Extinguisher to resolve.

Event

Patient Fainted

Emergency carry 锟?60s to get the fainted patient to a designated bed. Triggers from check-in stress, fire contact, or gunshot. Smelling Salts is the fast counter.

60s Timer

Ambulance Event 锟?Deep Dive

The Ambulance Event debuts at Shift 4 and re-fires every fifth shift. Six or more patients arrive near-simultaneously, all needing intake and most needing treatment immediately.

Triage Order

  1. Burning patients first 锟?60s timer is the tightest
  2. Critical Patient timers 锟?100-120s but needs full treatment chain
  3. Skinwalker conversions from any anomaly admitted in the panic
  4. Stable admissions 锟?last

The Cascade Problem

The trap is treating ambulance as "the same as a normal shift but faster." It isn't. Multiple emergencies can stack: Fire-in-Room + Patient Fainted + Critical Patient at once is plausible. The pattern: resolve the hazard that's still actively damaging things first, then the one that's just on a timer.

Pre-Shift 4 Loadout

  • Fire Extinguisher 锟?handles Patient on Fire and Fire-in-Room
  • Maple Syrup 锟?handles Bed Monster events
  • Coffee 锟?sanity restore between events
  • Large Speed Cola (if affordable) 锟?transit between cascading events

Death Ritual 锟?Counter Options

The Death Ritual is one of the most dramatic emergencies. A patient levitates surrounded by ritual candles with a ticking timer. You have multiple ways to cancel it:

  • Kick candles 锟?1-3 sanity cost per candle. Must extinguish ALL candles within the timer.
  • Tase patient 锟?no sanity loss, instant cancel. Requires Taser in inventory.
  • Apply Eye Drops 锟?cancels the ritual. Also works with IV Drops or Coffee.
  • Defibrillate 锟?fastest cancel if available in Room 7.
Note: The Death Ritual timer is reported as 30s by some sources and 60s by others. Patches may have changed it 锟?treat 30s as the safe planning number.
