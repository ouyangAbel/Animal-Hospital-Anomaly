Patient Treatment Workflow: The 6-Step Cheat Sheet

Every patient follows the same six-step workflow. Master this loop and you'll handle any condition the game throws at you.

Beginner Guide
June 20, 2026 | 7 min read

Every patient in Animal Hospital (Anomaly) follows the same six-step workflow. Master this loop and you'll handle any condition the game throws at you. The workflow is identical across all 5 shifts —?only the speed and anomaly density change.

The 6-Step Patient Workflow

  1. CHECK IN —?Approach the front desk. The patient appears at the admission window. Verify their ID photo against the live camera feed. Open the CCTV monitor. Snap a photo with the Photo Camera. If anything looks wrong —?reject the anomaly.
  2. OBSERVE —?Look at the patient's HUD indicator. Which condition is showing? Check for visual cues: coughing (Flu), bleeding (Bleeding), sunken eyes (Dehydration), red forehead (High Fever), etc. Cross-reference with the Condition —?Item Map.
  3. PROCURE —?Fetch the matching treatment item. Treatment items spawn free outside their relevant rooms. Check the Supply Room shelves or the Med-cart if the room stock is depleted.
  4. ADMINISTER —?Take the patient to the correct room and apply the item. IV Room for fluids, Surgery Room for stabilization, standard rooms for everything else. The Emergency Wing (Rooms 6-8) requires minigames before treatment completes.
  5. MONITOR —?Watch the patient's status gauge. Wait for it to cross the "Recovery" threshold. Don't leave the room during monitoring —?anomalies can manifest while you're distracted.
  6. DISCHARGE —?Once recovered, guide the patient to the exit door. They're done. Move to the next patient in the queue.

The Condition —?Item Quick Reference

Bookmark this. You'll memorize it in 10 minutes:

  • Bleeding —?Bandages
  • Bruises —?Medkit
  • Dehydration —?IV Bag (鈿狅—?wrong treatment = fatal)
  • Dried Eyes —?Eye Drops
  • Flu —?Cough Syrup
  • Headache —?Medicine
  • High Fever —?Cooling Gel
  • Low Sugar —?Maple Syrup
  • Rashes —?Ointment
  • Stomach Ache —?Herbs

Items With Dual Uses —?Know These

Three treatment items also function as off-label combat or event tools. Knowing these doubles your inventory efficiency:

  • Maple Syrup: treats Low Sugar AND bribes the Bed Monster. Always carry one.
  • Eye Drops: treats Dried Eyes AND pacifies Mass of Eyes. Also cancels Death Ritual.
  • Ointment: treats Rashes AND is the manual fallback for Patient on Fire when no Fire Extinguisher is in reach.

Where Items Live in the Hospital

  • Treatment items —?spawn free outside the relevant patient rooms
  • Fire Extinguisher —?wall-mounted glass cases near check-in booth and hallway stations
  • Photo Camera —?issued at the admission window from Shift 1
  • Mop —?Utility Closet
  • Shop items (Coffee, Taser, Speed Cola) —?Supplies Shop, unlocks after Shift 3

When Things Go Wrong

The workflow breaks down when anomalies appear mid-treatment. Here's the priority:

  1. Threat is active hostiles (Skinwalker, Hiders) —?Drop everything, grab weapon, fight or flee.
  2. Threat is passive drain (Mass of Eyes, Stalker) —?Break line of sight, continue treatment from a safe angle.
  3. Threat is timed emergency (Fire, Fainted, Death Ritual) —?Triage based on timer urgency. Emergency timers are your guide.
  4. No active threat —?Continue the 6-step workflow normally.
The workflow is simple. The difficulty comes from doing it under pressure while anomalies spawn, emergencies fire, and your sanity drops. Practice on Shifts 1-2 until the six steps are automatic. By Shift 4, you won't have time to think about them —?you'll just do them.
