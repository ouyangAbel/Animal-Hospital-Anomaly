Hiders

How to counter the Hiders enemy in Animal Hospital Anomaly. Wall-peeling detection, item theft, Fire Extinguisher and Taser counters.

Active
July 1, 2026

The Hiders are active enemies in Animal Hospital Anomaly. Flat humanoid shapes that peel off the walls, dash at the player, and steal inventory items. This guide covers how to detect Hiders, the Fire Extinguisher and Taser counters, and how to recover stolen items.

What Are the Hiders?

Hiders are flat humanoid shapes that disguise themselves as part of the wall. When you get close, they peel off the wall and dash at you, stealing items from your inventory. The stolen items are dropped on the ground where the Hider was standing.

Hiders are active enemies -- they actively pursue you and can steal critical items like Maple Syrup, Eye Drops, or Fire Extinguishers.

How to Detect Hiders

  • Look for flat shapes on walls -- Hiders appear as 2D humanoid silhouettes on wall surfaces.
  • Note the texture -- Hiders have a slightly different texture than the wall around them.
  • Watch for movement -- Hiders may shift or twitch slightly before peeling off.

The Fire Extinguisher and Taser Counters

There are two primary counters to Hiders:

  • Fire Extinguisher -- Spray the Hider to force it back into the wall. Free and unlimited.
  • Taser -- Tase the Hider to incapacitate it. Costs a Taser charge.

After counteracting the Hider, break line-of-sight by moving around a corner or closing a door. Hiders won't pursue you if they can't see you.

Recovering Stolen Items

If a Hider steals your items, they're dropped on the ground where the Hider was standing. Pick them up immediately before they despawn. The items are visible as glowing objects on the floor.

Counter-Strategy

The best counter is immediate reaction. When you encounter Hiders:

  1. Use Fire Extinguisher or Taser to counter the Hider
  2. Break line-of-sight by moving around a corner
  3. Recover any stolen items from the ground
  4. Continue with your normal workflow

Shift Appearances

Hiders can appear starting in Shift 2 and continue through higher shifts. They become more common in later shifts.
