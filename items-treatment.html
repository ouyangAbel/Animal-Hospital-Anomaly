Animal Hospital Anomaly Treatment Items Guide: All 10 Free Spawn Items & Condition Map

Every treatment item and what condition it cures — master the 10-item condition map in 10 minutes.

Items Guide
2026-07-01 | 9 min read

Treatment items are the backbone of every shift in Animal Hospital (Anomaly). There are exactly 10 treatment items, all spawning for free outside their relevant treatment rooms. No shop purchase is needed. This guide covers every item, what condition it treats, dual-use functionality, and the one mistake that can be fatal.

The Condition — Item Map

Every patient condition has exactly one correct treatment item. Memorize this table — it is small and you will have it down in 10 minutes:

  • Bleeding — Bandages
  • Bruises — Medkit
  • Dehydration — IV Bag
  • Dried Eyes — Eye Drops
  • Flu — Cough Syrup
  • Headache — Medicine
  • High Fever — Cooling Gel
  • Low Sugar — Maple Syrup
  • Rashes — Ointment
  • Stomach Ache — Herbs
Critical Warning: Dehydration is the exception — incorrect treatment is documented as fatal. If you are unsure, double-check the symptom before reaching for IV Bag. The wrong item on a Dehydrated patient can end a run.

Bandages

The dedicated counter for the Bleeding condition. Single-condition item with a clear primary use. Spawns outside the Bleeding treatment room. One of the most straightforward items in the game — no dual-use, no special mechanics. Just grab and apply.

Cough Syrup

Treats the Flu condition. Single-use respiratory medication stocked in standard cabinets. Spawns outside the Flu treatment room. No secondary uses — this is a pure treatment item.

Cooling Gel

Topical gel for the High Fever condition. Provides rapid surface body temperature reduction. Spawns outside the Fever treatment room. One of the last treatment items you will unlock in the treatment pipeline.

Eye Drops

A dual-use item and one of the most versatile in the game. Primary: treats Dried Eyes condition. Secondary: pacifies the Mass of Eyes anomaly when applied directly. Also works as a Death Ritual cancel option — applying Eye Drops to the levitating patient ends the ritual with zero sanity loss. Always worth carrying one.

Herbs

Herbal mixture for the Stomach Ache condition. Single-use digestive distress treatment. One of the first two Shift 1 patients always requires Herbs — this is a predictable pattern you can rely on for early-game efficiency.

IV Bag

Fluid replacement for Dehydration. This is the most dangerous treatment item in the game because it requires an IV stand setup and takes time to administer. The wrong treatment on Dehydration can be fatal — possibly the only condition where a wrong item kills the patient outright rather than just failing to heal. Always confirm Dehydration before picking up the IV Bag.

Maple Syrup

The most versatile treatment item in Animal Hospital Anomaly. Primary: treats the Low Sugar condition. Secondary: bribes the Bed Monster — approach any bed with a crimson box underneath holding Maple Syrup and the monster takes the bribe instead of grabbing you. This is the only counter for the Monster Eating Patient emergency. Always carry at least one Maple Syrup from Shift 1 onward.

Medicine

Generic pain reliever that treats the Headache condition. Standard cabinet stock. Single-purpose treatment item with no secondary uses.

Medkit

Treats Bruises — a blunt-trauma kit distinct from Bandages (which treat Bleeding) and Ointment (which treats Burns and Rashes). Easy to confuse with Bandages visually, but they treat different conditions. Check the label.

Ointment

A dual-use item. Primary: treats the Rashes condition. Secondary: serves as the manual fallback counter for Patient on Fire when no Fire Extinguisher is in reach. Slower than the extinguisher, but works. Spawns outside the treatment room.
