Animal Hospital Anomaly Monster & Enemy Guide

Every anomaly entity, hostile creature, and playable class detailed with detection methods, warning signs, and counter-strategies.

The hospital is crawling with horrors. This Animal Hospital Anomaly monster guide covers every entity you will encounter — from pre-admission anomalies that test your detection skills to floor enemies that actively hunt you. Understanding each creature's behavior is the key to survival.

Entities in Animal Hospital Anomaly are divided into two categories: Anomalies (pre-admission tells you act on at the window) and Enemies (post-admission threats on the hospital floor). Every missed anomaly transforms into a Skinwalker — the consequence is uniform regardless of which anomaly you missed.

Anomalies — Pre-Admission Detection

Anomalies are supernatural entities hiding among patients at the admission window. Each has unique warning signs and must be caught using one of three detection layers: Visual, Photo Camera, or CCTV Monitor. There are 21 documented anomalies in the game.

Visual-Class Anomalies (6)

Detected by looking directly at the patient at the admission window. No tools required — just your eyes.

Three Eyes

Three large eye sockets outlined by an orange glow. The easiest visual anomaly to spot — if you see a third eye, reject immediately.

Visual

Hollow Face

Patient with dark hollow eyes, twitching motions, and a hunched posture. Not always hostile, but guarantees a death ritual if admitted.

Visual

Wide Eyes and Creepy Smile

Abnormally large, wide-open eyes paired with a disturbing fixed smile. Obvious if you actually look at the face for a beat.

Visual

Mismatched Eyes and Sharp Teeth

Eyes sit at different heights paired with a sinister grin full of sharp teeth. Clear visual tell at the window.

Visual

Human Teeth

Animal patient renders with a full set of realistic human teeth. Distinct and unmistakable once you know to look.

Visual

Twitching

Watch the patient for 4-5 seconds — if they jerk their arms, neck, or upper body, refuse. Also has a CCTV-only variant (Camera Twitching).

Visual

CCTV-Class Anomalies (5)

Detected only on the security camera feed. These anomalies look normal at the window but reveal their true nature on the monitor.

Censored Eyes (Black Eyes)

Face obstructed by a black rectangular bar with realistic staring eyes visible underneath. Camera-only detection — the live view shows a normal patient.

CCTV

Skinwalker (Shapeshifter)

Appears as a normal patient at the window but shows its true form on the security camera. Once admitted, it turns hostile and must be fought with weapons. The most dangerous anomaly in the game.

CCTV

Void Body (Void/Black)

Patient body renders entirely black on the security camera. The strongest argument for always opening the CCTV feed.

CCTV

Unnatural Body (Distorted)

Heavily stretched and distorted body proportions on the security camera feed. Body shape changes each time CCTV is reopened.

CCTV

Staring at Cameras

Patient faces directly into the security camera lens continuously, even while moving. Camera-only detection — uncanny and obvious on the feed.

CCTV

Photo-Class Anomalies (6)

Detected only by using the Photo Camera. These anomalies appear normal both at the window and on CCTV, but the photo reveals hidden details. Costs 10 sanity per photo view.

Cursed Photo

Photographed patient shows bloodshot eyes or distorted smile. Viewing the photo drains 10 sanity plus additional cursed-photo cost.

Photo

Different Ears (Mismatching Ears)

Patient's ears or horns render differently on the security camera than in the live admission window. Requires cross-referencing photo with live view.

Photo

Different Eyes

Photo reveals missing pupils, altered eyelids, or eye details that differ from the live view. Subtle but catchable with careful observation.

Photo

Incorrect Photo

Photo shows a different animal species or markings than the patient at the window. 10 sanity per photo view.

Photo

Static Photo

Photo comes back with TV-like static obscuring the image. 10 sanity per view. Note: as of 6/22/26, static-only patients are now legitimate (not anomalies).

Photo

Unnatural Photo

Photo shows human teeth, uneven features, or other unnatural details absent from the live view. A catch-all for photo-detected anomalies.

Photo

Paperwork-Class Anomalies (2)

These anomalies defeat all three detection layers entirely. They require a different kind of effort — checking the appointment list or remembering recurring patients.

Missing Appointment

Patient is in the queue but doesn't appear on the appointment list. Visual, photo, and camera all clean. Check the appointment list against every queued patient.

Paperwork

Mimic Patient

Copies the appearance of a known recurring patient (most famously Ratthew). Detection by memory, not by paperwork. Remember what Ratthew looks like in Shifts 1-3.

Paperwork

Enemies — Post-Admission Hostiles

Enemies are hostile entities that spawn on the hospital floor during a shift. They don't all behave the same way — roughly three quarters are passive threats that damage you only if you engage with them incorrectly. The rest, most importantly Skinwalker, are active hostiles that pursue patients or staff.

Bed Monster

A crimson box appears under a patient bed — black hands and red eyes pull at whatever gets close. Counter: approach with Maple Syrup to bribe it. Always check under beds after admitting a patient.

Active

Skinwalker (Floor Enemy)

Patient-disguised enemy that turns hostile post-admission. Bite damage, sanity drain, weapons-only resolution. Counter: Gun, Taser, or Fire Extinguisher. Mash E to break the pin if grabbed.

Active

Mass of Eyes

A writhing cluster of eyes on the ceiling — drains sanity continuously while you're looking at it. Counter: Eye Drops or keep camera angled down. Never look up.

Passive

Ghost

Translucent floating entity added in the 6/26 patch. Drains sanity continuously while present, even when invisible. New and still being documented.

Passive

Head Banger

Hollow-eyed entity that smashes its head into the check-in window during Shifts 2-5. Counter: Coffee or food. Cheapest encounter resolution in the game.

Passive

Hiders

Flat humanoid shapes that peel off the walls, dash at the player, and steal inventory items. Counter: Fire Extinguisher or Taser, then break line-of-sight.

Active

Stalker

Once-per-run rat figure that lurks at corners. Looking directly at it costs 10 sanity, then it vanishes. Counter: avoid direct eye contact. Keep camera angled toward floor.

Passive

Surgery Monster / Tendril

Room 8 emergency event — purple tentacled blob on the surgery table. 45-second timer. Counter: complete the surgery minigame. Do NOT shoot or tase it — that kills the patient.

Event

Camera Figure

A black silhouette that pushes closer to the screen the longer you stay on the CCTV feed. Counter: close the CCTV feed before it gets too close.

Passive

Slime

An environmental hazard — a viscous puddle on the floor that damages on contact. Counter: Mop (hold E to clean). Free from Utility Closet.

Environmental

Playable Classes

Every class in Animal Hospital Anomaly — unlock cost, starting kit, special ability, and the playstyle it rewards. Choose wisely before each shift.

Free

Intern

The free starter class. No special abilities — pick this while learning the diagnosis flow. Default choice for new players.

Coins

Doctor

Sanity-on-cure — every correctly treated patient restores 1 sanity, plus bonus starting sanity at higher tiers. Strong passive for sustain.

Coins

Nurse

Cheapest paid class — buys you up to +3 inventory slots. The obvious first upgrade after Intern.

Coins

Secretary

Recovers 1 sanity per checked-in patient. The lobby-grinder class for high-volume intake shifts.

Coins

Paramedic

Built for movement — starts each shift with a Large Speed Cola (6 uses). For time-pressure plays and ambulance waves.

Coins

Psychologist

Doubles every sanity event — gains and losses both. 100% starting sanity. High-variance class for skilled players only.

Coins

Security

Defensive class — starts with a 5-use X-Taser. The Animal Coin combat anchor for hostile-heavy shifts.

Coins

Surgeon

Highest Animal Coin tier (2,500). Each successful cure grants sanity AND a temporary speed boost. Widely ranked as the strongest free class.

Robux

Head Nurse

Robux-exclusive (190 Robux) — starts with +3 inventory slots from shift one. The premium throughput-skip.

Robux

Secret Agent

Robux-exclusive combat class (320 Robux) — 20-use Gun at shift start. Highest firepower, but friendly-fire risk is real. Makes the game extremely easy in solo play.

Frequently Asked Questions

Anomalies are pre-admission tells you act on at the admission window — they test your detection skills. Enemies are post-admission threats that spawn on the hospital floor. Skinwalker exists on both sides because the admit-phase tell and the floor-combat encounter share the same entity.
Not all enemies can be killed. Passive threats like Mass of Eyes and Stalker are avoided or countered with specific items. Active hostiles like Skinwalker and Hiders can be defeated with weapons (Gun, Taser, Fire Extinguisher). Surgery Monster must be resolved by completing the minigame, not by attacking it.
Skinwalker is where most runs end. Either one that converted from a missed anomaly at intake, or one that caught you mid-treatment. Three counters work (Gun, Taser, Fire Extinguisher) but they're not interchangeable. If grabbed, mash E to break the pin — don't try to wait it out.
Intern is the default free starter class — use it while learning. Once you have coins, Nurse (+3 inventory slots) is the first upgrade. For long-term investment, save for Surgeon (2,500 coins) which is widely ranked as the strongest free class in the game.
The anomaly set expands with each shift. Shifts 1-3 introduce the basics (Bed Monster, Skinwalker, Mass of Eyes). Shift 4+ adds paperwork-class anomalies (Missing Appointment, Mimic Patient) and increases Skinwalker spawn frequency. Shift 7+ adds the Stalker.
