Hollow Face

How to detect and counter the Hollow Face anomaly in Animal Hospital Anomaly. Death ritual trigger, visual warning signs, and prevention tips.

Visual
July 1, 2026

The Hollow Face anomaly is one of the most dangerous Visual-Class anomalies in Animal Hospital Anomaly. Unlike other anomalies that transform into a Skinwalker, Hollow Face guarantees a death ritual if admitted. This guide covers how to detect it, what the death ritual entails, and how to prevent it entirely.

What Is the Hollow Face Anomaly?

Hollow Face presents as a patient with three distinct warning signs:

  • Dark hollow eyes -- The eye sockets appear as deep black voids that seem to absorb light rather than reflect them. They look like empty, bottomless pits in the patient's face.
  • Twitching motions -- The patient's body moves in jerky, unnatural ways. Arms twitch, head bobs, shoulders spasm. The movement is erratic and uncontrolled.
  • Hunched posture -- The patient leans forward with shoulders curved and head tilted down, as if something heavy presses on their back.

The combination of all three signs is the signature tell. Dark eyes alone might be normal rendering. Twitching alone might be a Twitching anomaly. But all three together -- dark eyes + twitching + hunched posture -- is Hollow Face.

How to Detect Hollow Face

  • Look for dark hollow eyes -- The eye sockets should appear deep black, almost like voids.
  • Watch for twitching movements -- The patient's arms, neck, or upper body will jerk erratically.
  • Check posture -- The patient hunches forward, shoulders curved, head tilted down.
  • Compare to normal patients -- Normal patients stand relatively still with normal eye rendering and upright posture.

The key challenge with Hollow Face is that the signs can be subtle under time pressure. Unlike Three Eyes with its bright orange glow, Hollow Face requires you to notice the absence of light in the eye sockets and the quality of the patient's movement.

What Happens If You Miss Hollow Face?

If you admit a Hollow Face patient, they don't transform into a Skinwalker. Instead, they trigger a death ritual -- a unique emergency event with its own timer and counter-mechanics:

  1. The patient levitates off the ground, surrounded by ritual candles
  2. A ticking timer starts (30-60 seconds depending on the shift)
  3. You must cancel the ritual before the timer expires, or the patient dies and your team takes massive sanity damage

Death Ritual Counter-Options (Ranked by Efficiency)

  1. Tase the patient -- No sanity loss, instant cancel. Requires Taser in inventory. Best option if available.
  2. Defibrillate -- Instant cancel. Requires access to Room 7 (Heart Monitor). Fastest if you're nearby.
  3. Apply Eye Drops / IV Drops / Coffee -- Cancels the ritual. Costs the item but no sanity loss. Good middle-ground option.
  4. Kick all candles -- 1-3 sanity cost per candle. Must extinguish ALL candles. Slowest and most sanity-costly. Last resort.

Counter-Strategy: Always Reject

The best counter is prevention. When the patient appears at the admission window:

  1. Look for dark hollow eyes -- deep black sockets that absorb light
  2. Watch for jerky, unnatural movements
  3. Check for hunched posture
  4. If you see all three signs, reject the patient immediately
  5. The rejection is clean -- no sanity cost, no consequences

Never gamble on Hollow Face. The death ritual is too costly and disruptive to risk. If you're even slightly unsure, reject. The cost of a false rejection is zero. The cost of a missed Hollow Face is potentially a team wipe.

Why Hollow Face Is Dangerous

Hollow Face is dangerous because it's not immediately obvious. The dark eyes and twitching can be subtle, especially when you're processing patients quickly. Players who rush through check-ins often miss it, leading to a death ritual that can cascade into a full team wipe. The death ritual also demands immediate attention -- you can't ignore it and continue treating other patients.
