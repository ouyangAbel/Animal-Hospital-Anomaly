Three Eyes

How to detect and counter the Three Eyes anomaly in Animal Hospital Anomaly. Visual warning signs, rejection strategy, and survival tips.

Visual
July 1, 2026

The Three Eyes anomaly is one of the six Visual-Class anomalies in Animal Hospital Anomaly. It appears at the admission window during patient check-in and is detectable without any tools -- just your eyes. This guide covers how to spot Three Eyes, what happens if you miss it, and the best counter-strategy.

What Is the Three Eyes Anomaly?

When a patient arrives at the admission window, look closely at their face. The Three Eyes anomaly presents as three large eye sockets arranged on the patient's face, with the third eye typically positioned on the forehead. Each socket is outlined by a distinctive orange glow that pulses slightly. The glow is the most reliable tell -- it's visible even in low-light conditions.

The third eye sits between and slightly above the normal pair. In most cases it's larger than the other two, making the asymmetry easy to catch once you know what to look for. The orange outline is not subtle -- think of it as a bright neon ring drawn around a third socket.

How to Detect Three Eyes

  • Visual check at the admission window -- This is the primary detection method. No tools required.
  • Look for the orange glow -- The third eye socket emits a bright orange outline that stands out against the patient's face.
  • Check the forehead area -- The extra eye is typically positioned between and slightly above the normal eyes.
  • Don't rush the check -- Spend 2-3 seconds examining each patient's face before admitting them.

Because the Three Eyes anomaly is purely visual, it falls into the easiest detection tier. You don't need to open the CCTV monitor or snap a Photo Camera shot. A careful 2-second look at the face is enough.

What Happens If You Miss Three Eyes?

If you fail to detect the Three Eyes anomaly and admit the patient, they will transform into a Skinwalker on the hospital floor. The Skinwalker is the most dangerous enemy in the game -- it will attack you and other staff members, dealing bite damage and draining sanity. You'll need to fight it with a Gun, Taser, or Fire Extinguisher, or prevent the transformation by rejecting the patient at the window.

The consequence is identical regardless of which anomaly you miss -- the patient always becomes a Skinwalker. But Three Eyes is the easiest anomaly to prevent, so missing it feels especially avoidable.

Counter-Strategy: Prevention Is Everything

The best counter to Three Eyes is rejection at the window. When the patient appears:

  1. Look at their face carefully for 2-3 seconds
  2. Check for the orange glow around a third eye socket
  3. If you see three eyes, reject the patient immediately
  4. The rejection is clean -- no sanity cost, no consequences

There is zero penalty for rejecting a Three Eyes patient. The only cost is the time it takes to spot the tell, and that's measured in seconds.

Why Three Eyes Is the Easiest Anomaly

Three Eyes is considered the easiest anomaly to detect because the visual tell is obvious and requires no tools. Unlike CCTV-only anomalies like Censored Eyes or Photo-class anomalies like Cursed Photo, Three Eyes can be spotted with a simple visual check. The bright orange glow is impossible to miss once you know to look for it.

This makes Three Eyes a great practice anomaly for new players learning the detection system. If you can consistently catch Three Eyes, you're ready to move on to the subtler visual tells like Hollow Face.

Shift Appearances

Three Eyes can appear in Shifts 1 through 5. It's one of the first anomalies new players encounter and remains a consistent threat throughout the early game. As you progress to higher shifts, the anomaly pool expands, but Three Eyes never disappears entirely.
