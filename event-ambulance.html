Ambulance Event Guide: Survive the 6+ Patient Surge in Animal Hospital Anomaly

Survive the Ambulance Event with our triage order, pre-shift loadout, and cascade management strategies.

Event Guide
2026-07-01 | 8 min read

The Ambulance Event is the most chaotic encounter in Animal Hospital (Anomaly). Debuting at Shift 4 and re-firing every fifth shift thereafter, it floods your hospital with 6+ patients simultaneously while Dr. Harlow announces an anomaly attack over the intercom. Multiple emergencies can stack on top of each other within the surge, creating a cascade that tests every skill you have developed.

When Does the Ambulance Event Trigger?

The first Ambulance Event always hits at Shift 4. After that, it recurs every 5 shifts like clockwork: Shift 9, Shift 14, Shift 19, and so on. Plan your loadout and sanity management around these intervals. If you are low on supplies or sanity heading into a multiple-of-5 shift, you are in trouble.

The Triage Order — Who to Treat First

When 6+ patients arrive simultaneously, you cannot treat everyone at once. Follow this triage order exactly:

  1. Burning patients first — The 60-second Patient on Fire timer is the tightest in the game. Fire Extinguisher immediately upon spotting flames.
  2. Critical Patient timers — 100-120 seconds but requires the full treatment chain (diagnose, procure, administer, monitor). Start these early because the chain takes time.
  3. Skinwalker conversions — Any anomaly you miss during the intake panic converts to a Skinwalker. Handle these as combat encounters. Taser or Fire Extinguisher.
  4. Stable admissions — Last. These patients can wait while you handle the active crises.

The Cascade Problem

The trap is treating the Ambulance Event as the same as a normal shift but faster. It is not. Multiple emergencies can fire simultaneously: Fire-in-Room + Patient Fainted + Critical Patient at once is plausible and has happened to experienced players. The key pattern: resolve the hazard that is actively damaging things first, then the one that is just on a timer. A burning room can knock standing patients into the fainted state, creating a spiral.

Pre-Ambulance Loadout Checklist

  • Fire Extinguisher — Non-negotiable. Handles Patient on Fire and Fire-in-Room. Refill at wall stations between waves.
  • Maple Syrup — Non-negotiable. Handles Bed Monster events during the chaos. Carry at least one.
  • Coffee (x2) — Sanity management during extended crisis. The Ambulance Event can drain 20-30 sanity points if things cascade.
  • Chocolate — Emergency sanity restore. Use when Coffee runs out.
  • Taser (5-use) — For Skinwalker conversions during the panic.
  • Large Speed Cola — If your budget allows. Transit speed between cascading emergencies makes a huge difference.

Common Failure Points

  • Skipping intake checks — The panic makes you want to speed through window checks. Do not. A missed anomaly becomes a Skinwalker that kills a patient and drains sanity.
  • Treating stable patients first — Triage order exists for a reason. Burning and Critical patients have hard timers. Stable patients can wait.
  • Empty inventory — Going into a known Ambulance shift with no Fire Extinguisher, no Maple Syrup, and no sanity items is a guaranteed wipe.
  • Ignoring sanity — The Ambulance Event drains sanity fast through multiple overlapping sources. If you drop below 30 sanity, you will start making worse decisions.
The Ambulance Event is not a test of speed. It is a test of triage. Know the priority order, carry the right items, and do not panic-check patients at the window.
