Mismatched Eyes and Sharp Teeth

How to detect and counter the Mismatched Eyes and Sharp Teeth anomaly in Animal Hospital Anomaly. Visual warning signs and rejection strategy.

Visual
July 1, 2026

The Mismatched Eyes and Sharp Teeth anomaly is one of the six Visual-Class anomalies in Animal Hospital Anomaly. It presents as a patient with eyes sitting at different heights paired with a sinister grin full of sharp teeth. This guide covers how to detect this anomaly, what happens if you miss it, and the best counter-strategy.

What Is the Mismatched Eyes and Sharp Teeth Anomaly?

This anomaly has two distinct visual tells:

  • Mismatched eyes -- The patient's eyes sit at noticeably different heights. One eye is higher than the other, creating an asymmetrical, unsettling look. The height difference is significant enough to be visible at a glance.
  • Sharp teeth -- The patient's mouth reveals a sinister grin full of pointed, predatory teeth. These are not normal animal teeth -- they're sharp, jagged, and designed to look dangerous.

The combination of lopsided eyes and menacing teeth makes this anomaly relatively easy to spot once you know what to look for. The asymmetry is the key -- normal patients have eyes at roughly the same height.

How to Detect Mismatched Eyes and Sharp Teeth

  • Compare eye heights -- Look at both eyes and check if one is noticeably higher than the other.
  • Check the teeth -- Look for sharp, pointed teeth in the patient's mouth.
  • Note the grin -- The patient often has a sinister, predatory expression.
  • Look for asymmetry -- The overall facial structure should look uneven and wrong.

What Happens If You Miss This Anomaly?

If you admit a patient with the Mismatched Eyes and Sharp Teeth anomaly, they will transform into a Skinwalker on the hospital floor. The Skinwalker is the most dangerous enemy in the game -- it will attack you and other staff members, dealing bite damage and draining sanity.

Counter-Strategy

The best counter is prevention. When the patient appears at the admission window:

  1. Compare the heights of both eyes -- look for asymmetry
  2. Check for sharp, pointed teeth
  3. Note any sinister or predatory expression
  4. If you see mismatched eyes and/or sharp teeth, reject the patient immediately
  5. The rejection is clean -- no sanity cost, no consequences

Shift Appearances

Mismatched Eyes and Sharp Teeth can appear starting in Shift 2 and continues through higher shifts. It's a mid-game anomaly that becomes more common as you progress.
